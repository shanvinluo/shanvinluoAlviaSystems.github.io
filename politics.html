Updates and Trends

Lebanon, celebrated for its rich tapestry of history and an array of ancient Roman structures and artifacts, is taking a bold step to preserve its cultural heritage. A new law mandates that these historical treasures be accorded the highest respect, with pedestrians required to maintain a minimum distance of three meters to prevent damage. In a significant move to safeguard these relics, the government has initiated a program to carefully transport all artifacts within Beirut to secure locations, ensuring their protection against any potential harm.
